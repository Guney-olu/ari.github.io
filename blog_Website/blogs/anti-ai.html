my recent observations and experiments with Nightshade have led me to question its true effectiveness in safeguarding our art(pxp). the intention behind Nightshade is simple, aiming to protect against model training at the size of sd-1.5, its execution leaves much to be desired.

Nightshade's impact on images can vary drastically depending on the intensity settings. At higher intensities, it introduces noticeable artifacts that are easily differentiable (kid stuff), defeating the purpose of subtle modification. Even at default settings, Nightshade fails to provide decent protection against model training while still altering the original content.

Furthermore, the ease with which the Nightshade watermark can be removed using tools like img2img at moderate denoise strengths (again kid stuff) raises concerns about its reliability as a long-term solution.

Despite claims that there are possible workarounds to counter Nightshade's effects, its overall effectiveness remains dubious. As such, I cannot in good conscience recommend its widespread adoption, especially to those seeking foolproof protection for their digital creations or ART.
